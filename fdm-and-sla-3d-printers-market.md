<p><p>Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) and Stereolithography (SLA) are popular 3D printing technologies widely used across various industries. FDM printers work by melting thermoplastic filament and extruding it layer by layer to create a three-dimensional object. This technology is favored for its simplicity, cost-effectiveness, and versatility in material use. In contrast, SLA utilizes a UV laser to cure liquid resin into hardened plastic, allowing for finer details and smoother finishes, making it popular in applications requiring higher precision, such as dental and jewelry industries.</p><p>The FDM and SLA 3D Printers Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.9% during the forecast period, driven by advancements in technology and increasing demand for rapid prototyping across sectors such as aerospace, automotive, healthcare, and consumer goods. Key trends include the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning to enhance printing efficiency and quality, along with the rise of biocompatible materials for medical applications.
