<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are categorized into two primary types for electricity generation: multi-walled (MWCNTs) and single-walled (SWCNTs). MWCNTs consist of multiple concentric nanotubes, offering enhanced electrical conductivity and structural strength, making them ideal for applications like electrodes and energy storage devices. In contrast, SWCNTs, composed of a single tubular structure, exhibit exceptional electrical properties and high surface area, making them suitable for advanced photovoltaic cells and nanoscale electronics. Both types significantly enhance the efficiency of electricity generation technologies.</p></p>
